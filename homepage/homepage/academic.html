General Overview

Name: Faridpur High School

Location: Faridpur Sadar, Faridpur District, Bangladesh

Established: 1889

Type: Government Secondary School

Grades: Classes 6 to 10

Medium of Instruction: Bengali

School Board: Board of Intermediate and Secondary Education, Dhaka

EIIN: 108729

Academic Curriculum

Faridpur High School follows the national curriculum prescribed by the National Curriculum and Textbook Board (NCTB) of Bangladesh. The school offers education from Class 6 to Class 10, preparing students for the Secondary School Certificate (SSC) examination. The curriculum includes core subjects such as Bangla, English, Mathematics, Science, Bangladesh and Global Studies, Information and Communication Technology (ICT), Religion, and Physical Education.

In Classes 9 and 10, students choose one of the following academic streams:

  • Science
  • Business Studies (Commerce)
  • Humanities (Arts)

Each stream includes specialized subjects alongside the core curriculum, aligning with the SSC examination requirements.

Academic Features

Curriculum Structure

Classes 6 to 8: Students follow a uniform curriculum that includes core subjects such as Bangla, English, Mathematics, Science, Bangladesh and Global Studies, Information and Communication Technology (ICT), Religion, Arts and Crafts, Physical Education, and Work and Life-Oriented Education.

Classes 9 and 10: Students choose one of three academic streams—Science, Business Studies (Commerce), or Humanities (Arts)—each comprising specialized subjects alongside core subjects.

Assessment and Evaluation

The school conducts regular assessments, including class tests, term examinations, and annual examinations, to evaluate student progress.

Students in Class 10 appear for the Secondary School Certificate (SSC) examination, administered by the Board of Intermediate and Secondary Education, Dhaka.

Extracurricular Activities

Faridpur High School emphasizes the holistic development of students by encouraging participation in various extracurricular activities:

  • Sports: The school offers facilities for sports such as cricket, football, badminton, and handball, promoting physical fitness and teamwork.
  • Cultural Programs: Students engage in cultural events, including music, dance, drama, and art competitions, fostering creativity and cultural appreciation.
  • Debate and Public Speaking: The school organizes debate competitions and public speaking events to enhance students' communication skills and critical thinking.
  • Science Fairs and Exhibitions: Participation in science fairs and exhibitions encourages scientific inquiry and innovation among students.
  • Clubs and Societies: Various clubs, such as the Green Club, provide platforms for students to pursue interests in environmental awareness and community service.
  • Interactive Computing Club: A club focused on developing computing skills, coding, and interactive technology projects.
